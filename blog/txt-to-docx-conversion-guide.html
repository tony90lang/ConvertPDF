A Beginner's Guide to Converting TXT to DOCX (Preserve Your Formatting)

Published on March 3, 2026 | 📁 Text Files, Word Documents

Plain text files (.txt) are simple, universal, and lightweight – but they lack any formatting. No bold, no italics, no fonts, no line spacing. When you need to share a document professionally, you need a Word file (.docx).

In this guide, we'll explore how to convert TXT to DOCX while adding the formatting you want. Whether you're turning notes into a report or converting code comments into documentation, we've got you covered.

Why Convert TXT to DOCX?

  • Professional appearance: DOCX supports fonts, styles, headings, and images.
  • Compatibility: Microsoft Word is the industry standard for document sharing.
  • Accessibility: Screen readers and assistive tools work better with structured Word documents.
  • Editing flexibility: Recipients can easily edit and add comments.

Method 1: Using Microsoft Word (Manual)

  1. Open Microsoft Word.
  2. Go to File → Open and select your .txt file.
  3. Word will open it, but you'll need to manually apply styles – fonts, headings, line spacing – which can be tedious.
  4. Save as .docx.

This works, but it's time‑consuming, especially for long documents.

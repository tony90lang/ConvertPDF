Merging PDFs Online – Why You Should Avoid Uploading Sensitive Files

Published on March 3, 2026 | 📁 PDF, Privacy, Security

Merging multiple PDFs into one document is a common task. Whether it's combining invoices, merging research papers, or consolidating contracts, a good PDF merger saves time and hassle.

But here's the problem: most online PDF mergers require you to upload your files to a server. That means your confidential documents – contracts, financial statements, personal data – are sitting on someone else's computer, potentially at risk of being hacked, leaked, or misused.

In this guide, we'll explore the risks and introduce a privacy‑first alternative that keeps your files safe.

The Hidden Dangers of Upload‑Based PDF Mergers

When you use a typical online PDF tool, you're trusting the service provider with your data. Risks include:

  • Data breaches: If their server is hacked, your documents could be exposed.
  • Data retention: Some services keep copies of your files for "analysis" or "improvement."
  • Legal liability: If you're handling client documents, uploading them may violate confidentiality agreements.
  • File size limits: Most free services restrict file sizes, forcing you to pay for larger documents.

A 2024 study showed that 100 million Americans had their medical records leaked in a single breach – many from unsecured file uploads. Don't let your documents be next.

The Privacy‑First Alternative: Client‑Side PDF Merging

What if you could merge PDFs without uploading them anywhere? That's exactly how ConvertPDF works. All processing happens locally in your browser, using the pdf-lib library.

Technical How‑It‑Works

ConvertPDF uses the pdf-lib library to process PDFs entirely in the browser. When you select files, they're read into memory using the File API, merged locally, and then downloaded as a new Blob. No data is ever transmitted over the network.

You can even verify this yourself: open your browser's Developer Tools (F12), go to the Network tab, and watch – no requests to external servers.
